Who should use this site?

State and Territory AT Programs

State and Territory AT Programs can compare their own program scope and distribution to other programs and national aggregate data and identify similarities and differences for program evaluation, improvement and development initiatives.

Policymakers

Policymakers can understand the scope of activities conducted by individual State and Territory AT Programs and measure national aggregate impact across multiple years.

Researchers and academics

Researchers and academics can utilize standard reports for rapid analyses or select individual data elements and download spreadsheets to drill down on specific information of interest.

Individuals with disabilities and their families

Individuals with disabilities and their families can learn about the activities a State/Territory AT Program conducts and how to access those programs.

Advocates

Advocates can access consumer success stories and innovative initiatives to advocate on behalf of their State/Territory AT Programs and influence decision-makers.

ABOUT CATADA

State and Territory Assistive Technology Programs focus on improving the provision of Assistive Technology (AT) through comprehensive, statewide programs that are consumer responsive. The goal of these programs is to increase access to and acquisition of AT. Programs serve people with all types of disabilities, of all ages, in all environments.

WHAT IS AT?

AT is any item, piece of equipment, or system, whether acquired commercially, modified, or customized, that is commonly used to increase, maintain, or improve functional capabilities of individuals with disabilities.
